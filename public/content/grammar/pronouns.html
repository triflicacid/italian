Pronouns

Subject Pronouns

These are the words that normally appear at the start of a sentence before a verb, and communicate who the subject of the action is.

Number Italiano Inglese
Sg. Io I
Tu You
Lui/Lei He/She
Pl. Noi We
Voi You
Loro They

The pronoun may be omitted unless

  • To avoid ambiguity (he/she or irregular verbs)
  • It is modified with anche (also/too)
  • Emphasis or clarity

Examples

Mangio il panini
I eat the sandwhich

Lui beve la bottiglia d'acqua
He drinks the bottle of water

Direct Object Pronouns

These are pronouns which are used as substitues for the name of a person or object.

Number Italiano Inglese
Sg. Mi Me
Ti You
Lo Him
La Her
Pl. Ci Us
Vi You
Li Them (masc.)
Le Them (fem.)

They always precede a transitive verbs (verbs that are followed by an object), such as

  • Mangiare - to eat
  • Capire - to understand
  • Vedere - to see
  • Sapere - to know

Examples

Mi vedi? Vedo tu.
[Can] you see me? I see you.

No, non lo conosco
I don't know him

Indirect object pronouns

Pronouns used for the person or object the thing is being done to/for.

Number Italiano Inglese
Sg. Mi To/for me
Ti To/for you
Gli To/for him
Le To/for her
Pl. Ci To/for us
Vi To/for you
Loro To/for them

Examples

Gli parliamo?
Shall we talk to him?
